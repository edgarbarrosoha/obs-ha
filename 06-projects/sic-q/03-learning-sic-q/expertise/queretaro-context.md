# Expertise: Querétaro Context

## Domain
Local political, social, and institutional context for SIC-Q implementation in Querétaro, Mexico.

## Geographic Context

- **State:** Querétaro, Mexico
- **Population:** ~2.4 million (2025 estimate)
- **Capital:** Santiago de Querétaro
- **Municipalities:** 18
- **Economic profile:** Industrial corridor, aerospace, automotive, services

## Political Context

### Government Structure
- Governor (6-year term, no re-election)
- State Congress (25 deputies)
- 18 Municipal Presidents
- CEPACI (Center for Participation and Citizen Consultation)

### Political Cycle Awareness
SIC-Q must transcend political cycles through:
- Instituto del Futuro (independent governance)
- Long-term vision (30 years)
- Institutional embedding beyond administration changes

### Current Administration
- Focus on citizen participation (favorable environment)
- Digital government initiatives
- Interest in AI/innovation

## Institutional Partners

| Institution | Role in SIC-Q |
|-------------|---------------|
| CEPACI | Primary client, citizen consultation mandate |
| Government of Querétaro | Sponsor, policy implementation |
| Instituto del Futuro | Long-term governance (proposed) |
| Universities | Research, validation, youth engagement |
| Civil society | Legitimacy, outreach, monitoring |

## Existing Participation Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Status | Gap SIC-Q Addresses |
|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| Public consultations | Active | No AI synthesis |
| Suggestion boxes | Limited | No feedback loop |
| Town halls | Periodic | Not scalable |
| Social media | Informal | No structure |

## Demographic Considerations

### Digital Access
- Urban: High smartphone penetration
- Rural: WhatsApp as primary channel
- Youth: Digital-native, social media active
- Elderly: May need in-person support

### Language
- Spanish (primary)
- Some indigenous languages (Otomí)
- Technical English for international content

### Socioeconomic
- Growing middle class
- Industrial workforce
- Agricultural communities
- University students

## Cultural Factors

- Strong civic identity ("Queretanos")
- History of peaceful political culture
- Business-friendly environment
- Growing tech sector

## Risks and Sensitivities

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Political instrumentalization | Instituto del Futuro independence |
| Elite capture | Multi-channel access (WhatsApp, in-person) |
| Low rural participation | Community outreach, local facilitators |
| Distrust of government | Transparent feedback loops |
