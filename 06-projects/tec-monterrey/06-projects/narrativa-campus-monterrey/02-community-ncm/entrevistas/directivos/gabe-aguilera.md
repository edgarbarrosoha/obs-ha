School of Social Sciences and Government

Dean Gabriel Aguilera

· How do you envision success for your area in 2030?

Considerable Improvements in Human Capital: Faculty

 That we significantly improve the quality of our research and teaching faculty;

 That we increase the number of permanent faculty engaged in research, I recommend 2-3 new faculty per year for the region for five years, including two new faculty of excellence (university professors) for Monterrey;

 That we increase the quality of our part time faculty;

 That the quantity and quality of our scholarly peer-reviewed publications improve, this together with the quality of high-impact policy reports;

 That our faculty continue to increase research and consulting grants for high impact applied research

 That our faculty of political science be recognized as the best in all of Latin America;

 That our faculty of economics be considered in the top 5 of all of Latin America;

 That are law department be considered among the top 5 in Mexico.

Considerable Improvements in Human Capital: Students

 That we grow both the quantity and quality of our new students. Currently we have around 240 new students that enter our school per year.

 That by 2030, we hope to have in the neighborhood of 300 or so new students per year;

 That the proportion of high-quality students, as measured by institutional criteria, should continue to increase, which has been the case in recent years;

 That a greater proportion of our students participate in research and projects with our faculty, leading to greater numbers of students actively participating in research and publications;

 That greater proportions of our students, within 5 years of graduation, attend top graduate programs in public policy and administration, law, political science, international affairs, and business.

· What key partnerships will be essential to achieve it?

 We will need support from our provost to authorize new faculty positions.

 We will need support from our campus VPO so that we can continue to improve our efforts at student recruitment.

 We will need support from our National Dean so that we can hire additional staff for our recruitment efforts.

 We will need support from our human resources authorities so that we can continue to recruit and hire faculty in a timely and effective way.

 We need to continue to enhance our network at top universities around the world so we can recruit even more effectively, particularly in law and economics where we face stiffer challenges.

· What skills do you need to strengthen or develop?

 I need to steadily improve my communications skills, particularly with respect to presenting at public events, in order to more effectively “sell” our school and programs.

· How can digital tools and AI serve as enablers?

 I need more practice in putting AI to work to help me with my public presentations. Spanish is not my first language.

 Our school’s efforts need to improve so that we can require our faculty to put AI to use effectively, and ethically, in the classroom.

· What external and contextual data should we be paying close attention to? (e.g., socioeconomic trends, future of work analysis, political landscape, etc.)

 The geopolitical challenges resulting from President Donald Trump’s reckless foreign policy

 The potential for a global financial markets, that might flinch due to geopolitical factors and or a global financial bubble

 That advancement of AI and technology displacing workers and managers

· What are your priority initiatives to trigger change in 2026?

 Comprehensive plans to implement our new academic programs that will commence in Fall 2026

 Efforts to persuade our national dean to request and obtain new faculty lines

 Our efforts to upgrade and improve our outreach efforts to attract new students during the next 5 years.
