**What is TE?**

---

**TRANSDISCIPLINARY ENGINEERING** 

  

Today’s complex problems, especially those that have a large impact on the environment and society, require a holistic approach to solving them by considering knowledge from a diverse range of disciplines, technical as well as social, and from user and practitioner communities. Interchange of such cross-disciplinary knowledge is needed to determine the overall impact on the environment and society. Many examples exist of engineering innovations that have a large adverse effect on the environment by not considering measures and regulations for mitigating these effects, for example, non-biodegradable plastics, certain pesticides and fertilizing products, use of greenhouse gasses, the long-term effect of medication and medical procedures, etc.

  

To create engineering solutions that minimize environmental and societal damage, engineering design and practices must involve people with the necessary knowledge and methods to determine the impact of new products and services, not only on client and user satisfaction but also on long-term sustainability. Designers and engineers need to come from a wide range of disciplines to achieve such sustainable solutions. The support of advanced ICT technology is deemed indispensable.

  

[The International Society of Transdisciplinary Engineering (ISTE)](https://intsoctransde.org/) aims to create awareness that engineering practices need to evolve into transdisciplinary engineering practices in which the exchange of different types of knowledge and people from a diverse range of disciplines is fundamental.

  

Transdisciplinary Engineering (TE) is the exchange of knowledge in the context of innovation, in product, process, organization, or social environment. Through ISTE, the exchange of knowledge is possible in its annual conferences, the TE Conferences, and through the publication of special issues in scientific journals.

**Conference Theme**

---

**Transdisciplinarity for a better world**

  

Since 1994 our [ISTE](https://intsoctransde.org/) community has convened annually to discuss engineering for today’s most pressing social and technical challenges. The next TE conference will be held in the summer of 2025 on the EGADE Business School, of Tecnologico de Monterrey, in Monterrey, Mexico.

  

 The world is changing rapidly. The connectedness of the world’s most critical systems along with rapid advancement of methods push us to ask “How will we teach, research, and practice to engineer a better world?” The recent decade has shown an ongoing shift towards model-based design, instrumented products and teams, data-driven analytics, rapid prototyping, and continuous delivery. These changes are enabled on digital and physical platforms driven by distributed teams and open interfaces. Transformations are underway in several industrial sectors, including energy, agriculture, health, education, government, civil organizations, transportation among others always with a sustanable vision. It is an exciting time to be an transdisciplinar world changer!. 

  

We recognize the insufficiency of simple boundary crossing amongst traditional disciplines, as transdisciplinar professionals move beyond disciplinary norms established in the mid to late 20th century. Today we address systems in their context across a life-cycle, collaborate with a myriad of stakeholders and engineering and non-engineering academics and practitioner experts. 

  

With this framework in mind, TE2025 summon all transdisciplinar academic and non academic professionals that seek a better world by solving real complex problems, by taking into account stakeholders, using quantitative along with non quantitative disciplines, involving practitioners and academic point of views to join us in this conference.

{*Horizons Architecture: A Transdisciplinary Framework for Complex Systems and Hybrid Intelligence*

In an era of heightened complexity, systemic interdependence, and uncertainty, there is a growing interest in transdisciplinary frameworks that can adapt across diverse contexts. *Horizons Architecture (HA)* offers a *systemic and computational approach* that integrates *systems thinking, generative and agentic artificial intelligence, and fractal knowledge structures* to inform decision-making, problem-solving, and strategic foresight across multiple sectors. By embedding knowledge within dynamically evolving, multi-agent environments, HA provides a meta-structure designed to coordinate human and artificial intelligence in real-time, addressing wicked problems across various domains.

At its core, HA is organized around a dual-axis model that combines simultaneous complexity—operationalized through six fixed taxonomic dimensions (Legacy, Community, Learning, Technology, Context, and Projects)—with a generative data structure and an agentic ontology.

This presentation will outline both the theoretical aspects of HA and its potential real-world applications, examining its capacity to integrate diverse knowledge systems, enable emergent intelligence, and support the coordination of complex initiatives. A key example is its use in:

•⁠  ⁠*State-Level Public Governance*: HA has been applied to align a state-wide government program in Guanajuato, supporting multi-stakeholder decision-making. Through policy feedback loops and AI-driven scenario generation, the framework seeks to optimize governance in rapidly changing environments.

By positioning knowledge as a recursive, self-referential network, HA diverges from reductionist, discipline-specific models, instead employing transdisciplinary principles. In doing so, HA aspires to foster a high-dimensional, adaptive approach that strikes a balance between theoretical rigor and practical scalability.

This session will discuss how fractal knowledge architectures, multi-agent AI ecosystems, and transdisciplinary methods can reframe our understanding of complexity, intelligence, and organizational transformation. Rather than serving as a static framework, HA proposes **an evolving framework for complex endeavors transformations*, while acknowledging the need for continued empirical examination and iterative refinement.
