### I. Generales (General Overview)

talisis is positioned as a **catalytic platform** that aims to transform communities through integral educational empowerment, operating as a fractal ecosystem connecting technology, humanity, and social purpose. Its fundamental purpose is to offer **accessible and pertinent education** that generates sustainable social mobility and virtuous cycles of transgenerational development.

The organisation encompasses a multi-brand ecosystem that includes **Universidad Interamericana para el Desarrollo (UNID)**, **Universidad Regiomontana (UR)**, **Harmon Hall**, **Advenio**, and **IESalud**. It has a significant national presence, with **190 points of learning** across various locations, serving approximately **150,000 active students annually** and boasting over **2 million graduates**.

Leaders across different areas, such as Alberto Sánchez (Planning), Amalia Ortega (HRBP), and Juan Arenas (COO), consistently highlight education as the base for a better Mexico and a key factor for social transformation. The vision for 2035 is ambitious, aiming for **international impact**, becoming a global leader in educational transformation, and being a key strategic partner for organisations and governments in talent development.

### II. Lo que distingue a TALISIS (What distinguishes TALISIS)

talisis distinguishes itself through several key elements:

- **Adaptive and Flexible Educational Model**: talisis aims to be "pedagogically agnostic," meaning its pedagogy evolves organically, adapting to unique student needs, community challenges, and market demands. This includes combining the dual German model with Mexican business projects, integrating 21,000 online courses with immersive in-person experiences, and creating safe spaces for experimentation. The goal is to offer education that is flexible, accessible, and of high quality, reaching diverse communities through multiple brands and channels.
- **Student-Centric Approach**: The core philosophy is to place the student at the centre of all decisions and processes, from admission to graduation and beyond, ensuring a smooth, valuable, and consistent experience. This commitment extends to understanding the evolving needs of students across different life stages (from early childhood to postgraduate and lifelong learning).
- **Technological Innovation as an Enabler**: Technology, especially Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Cloud computing, is seen not as a replacement but as an amplifier of human capabilities. The "talisis mind" is envisioned as a central brain that personalises, analyses, and enhances learning, creating a human-machine symbiosis. Projects include AI agents for student/staff support, predictive analytics to reduce attrition, and unified technological architecture. The organisation is actively exploring AI for content creation (Project Mazinger Z), automated marketing (hyper-personalisation, predictive analysis), and optimising internal processes like compensation calculations.
- **Focus on Employability and Competencies**: talisis aims to equip students with the necessary knowledge and skills for a constantly changing professional world, including 21st-century competencies. This involves aligning educational offerings with industry needs and market trends, ensuring graduates are prepared for current and future job markets.
- **Culture of Development and High Performance**: There is a strong emphasis on fostering a culture of continuous learning, professional development, and high performance within the organisation itself. This includes empowering leaders, promoting a sense of ownership ("mentalidad de dueño"), and ensuring internal processes support a healthy and productive environment.
- **Strategic Alliances and Global Vision**: talisis actively seeks strategic alliances with other academic institutions (national and international), government bodies, and leading companies to exchange ideas, align educational offerings with governmental development projects, and ensure graduates meet industry demands. Its presence in 7 countries and vision for Latin American leadership underscores its global ambition while respecting local pertinence.

### III. Principales retos y oportunidades. ¿Qué les quita el sueño? (Main challenges and opportunities. What keeps them awake at night?)

**A. Challenges (What keeps them awake at night):**

- **Internal Operational and Process Deficiencies:**
    - **Lack of Documentation and Clarity**: Many processes are not documented or are unclear, leading to inefficiencies, emergencies, and hindering planning.
    - **System Fragmentation and Data Inconsistencies**: There's a "lack of integration between systems" (e.g., Banner and SAP), leading to "open controls" and disorganisation. Multiple isolated systems cause data inconsistencies, making it hard to get a unified view of information.
    - **Manual and Bureaucratic Processes**: Many tasks are still manual, consuming significant time and resources (e.g., financial reconciliations, reporting, payroll calculations for promoters). Bureaucratic processes hinder commercial efforts and overall agility.
    - **Operational Overload**: Rapid expansion can lead to overloads in areas like faculty, support, and attention, potentially compromising student experience.
    - **Difficulty in Global/Collaborative Projects**: The organisation struggles to action global or cross-functional projects effectively, leading to desynchronisation and duplicated efforts.
- **Cultural and Organisational Resistance:**
    - **Resistance to Change**: There's internal resistance to new ways of thinking and operating, especially concerning digital transformation and innovation.
    - **Lack of Digital Mindset/Capabilities**: Not all employees possess the necessary digital skills, or perceive online education as a competitor rather than an ally.
    - **Leadership Maturity**: Developing leaders with the necessary maturity and a culture of talent development takes time.
    - **Siloed Operations**: Different areas (commercial, IT, academic, operations, marketing) often operate in silos with misaligned priorities, hindering collaboration and shared vision.
- **Resource Constraints:**
    - **Limited Resources (Time, People, Budget)**: Teams are often small and overloaded, limiting time for strategic planning, design, and innovation. There's a need for more human resources, particularly technical specialists.
    - **Budget Limitations**: Affects hiring leadership for laboratories and general expansion.
- **Talent Management and Development:**
    - **High Employee Turnover**: Particularly in high-turnover positions like sales advisors, making it difficult to retain talent.
    - **Skill Gaps**: Need to continuously update team skills in technology, data analysis, and strategic thinking.
    - **Lack of Clear Teacher Profile/Development**: Historical lack of a dedicated HR area for teachers, leading to less intentional profile design and development.
- **External Market and Economic Factors:**
    - **Market Saturation & Fierce Competition**: The educational market is increasingly saturated with many institutions, formats, and promises, making differentiation difficult. Emergence of new EdTech players intensifies competition.
    - **Economic Environment**: A "complicated and recessive economic environment" directly impacts families' ability to pay for education, affecting new enrolments and student retention. Managing cash flow and recovering overdue accounts are critical short-term financial priorities.
    - **Regulatory Changes**: New labour regulations (e.g., move to 40-hour work weeks) and stricter data privacy rules require continuous adaptation. Political and socio-political factors, including union issues, can also affect operations and productivity.
    - **Changing Perception of Education**: Some individuals believe a university degree is no longer the sole path to career success, challenging the value proposition of formal education.
    - **Digital Fatigue and Authenticity**: Audiences are experiencing digital fatigue and demand more human, purpose-driven, and authentic communication from brands.
    - **Low Culture of Training in Mexico**: A significant challenge is overcoming the low culture of continuous training or talent development within Mexican companies (both national and foreign).

**B. Opportunities:**

- **Leveraging Technology for Scale and Personalisation:**
    - **AI and Cloud Transformation**: Significant potential to simplify daily activities, automate processes (e.g., financial reconciliations, compensation calculations, recruitment), gain deeper insights from data, and enable hyper-personalisation of learning experiences.
    - **Big Data and Predictive Analytics**: Opportunity to build a robust data architecture for a 360-degree view of students, enabling early detection of risks (e.g., attrition), personalised support, and tailored educational offerings.
    - **Digital Transformation**: Position talisis as a leader in educational innovation in Mexico and Latin America by embracing digital models and integrating technology into all aspects of the educational ecosystem.
- **Market Expansion and Diversification:**
    - **International Reach**: Expand into other Spanish-speaking countries and potentially globally, leveraging AI for content customisation and language adaptation.
    - **New Geographic Models**: Expand strategically with new, financially viable models like hybrid hubs or "talisis Studios" that offer physical presence for interaction and sales without the full investment of a campus.
    - **Nearshoring and Tourism**: Capitalise on the nearshoring phenomenon in northern Mexico by training local and international labour for new industries. Develop professionals for the tourism sector, especially with upcoming major events like the North American World Cup.
    - **Educational Diversification**: Expand portfolio with new programmes (e.g., technical, health-related, micro-credentials) and modalities (online, hybrid, lifelong learning) to meet evolving market demands and adapt to an uncertain professional future.
- **Strengthening Alliances and Ecosystem**:
    - **Strategic Partnerships**: Forge alliances with leading companies (including Fortune 500), government bodies, international organisations (e.g., IDB, UN, UNESCO), and EdTech companies to co-create content, offer employability opportunities, drive innovation, and align with public development initiatives.
    - **Community Building**: Create authentic learning communities and engage students as active participants and ambassadors, moving beyond traditional marketing to build trust and long-term relationships.
- **Organisational Development**:
    - **Talent Development**: Opportunity to develop internal talent and leadership, focusing on a strong culture of transformation and high performance.
    - **Process Optimisation**: Standardise and automate processes to improve efficiency, reduce "emergencies," and allow teams to focus on strategic, high-value activities.
    - **Branding and Positioning**: Strengthen brand positioning and differentiation with clear, emotional, and segmented value propositions, building trust and reputation.

### IV. Objetivos y Proyectos/Iniciativas Estratégicas (Objectives and Strategic Projects/Initiatives)

talisis's overarching objective for 2035 is to be a **leading educational group with the greatest reach and accessibility in Mexico, and potentially internationally**, offering integral solutions that are adapted to industry needs and provide personalised development for professional goals.

Key strategic projects and initiatives, often interconnected, are:

- **talisis Bit (Business Management Plan)**: A central, transversal initiative led by Joel Enriquez and Mariel Serrano, aimed at aligning and synchronising all areas of the company. It's described as "the pulse, the sequence, the frequency" for over 1500 people to work with focus, rhythm, and common sense. Its purpose is to build and maintain a solid reputation and prestige, emphasising that "reputation starts from within". It's a long-term project focused on discipline and vision aligning with culture and strategy.
- **High-Performance Culture**: A top priority for Human Capital, focusing on attracting and developing the best talent, setting high standards, and ensuring a healthy culture with strong leadership. This includes rigorous staffing processes and clear role definitions.
- **Unified Data Architecture and Analytics**: Build a robust data architecture (data lake) to collect comprehensive information about students, teachers, and administrative staff. This will enable a 360-degree view of clients, provide real-time dashboards, and support data-driven decision-making, moving away from fragmented data and manual reporting.
- **AI and Cloud Integration**: Implement AI agents (e.g., chatbots, virtual assistants) for 24/7 student support, administrative task automation, and predictive analytics for early risk detection. This also involves leveraging cloud technology for scalability, data availability, and security.
- **Academic Model Homologation and Innovation**:
    - **Micro-credentials System**: Create a system of micro-credentials to offer flexible, adaptable learning pathways that respond to evolving market needs and prepare students for multiple job changes throughout their careers.
    - **Curricular Homologation**: Standardise the curriculum across brands where appropriate, while allowing for local adaptation.
    - **Redefining Teaching Roles**: Transform the role of teachers into facilitators of critical thinking and socialisation, rather than just technical content providers, adapting to how students learn with technology.
- **Online and Hybrid Model Expansion**: Scale the "vivo virtual" model and explore new hybrid models (e.g., hubs, franchises in strategic cities) to increase reach and accessibility, especially in areas with less investment required than full campuses.
- **Omnichannel Strategy**: Implement an integrated omnichannel strategy for communication and sales, ensuring a consistent and coherent experience across digital and physical touchpoints, and leveraging technology for automation and personalisation of the customer journey.
- **Product and Brand Positioning**: Redesign and professionalise the online product offering. Strengthen brand positioning with clear, relevant, and differentiated narratives for each talisis brand, aligning them with the 2035 vision and building reputation.
- **Teacher Development and Career Path**: Implement a clear career path for teachers, offering continuous professional development and identifying top performers, to improve quality and retention.
- **Commercial Productivity Improvement**: Automate and improve sales processes (e.g., lead nurturing, forecasting). Improve sales force productivity, reduce recruitment time for high-turnover positions, and offer better visibility on compensation.
- **Quality Management System**: Consolidate a robust quality management system that ensures academic content and processes meet high standards, aligning with accreditations (like FINPES) and fostering continuous improvement cycles. This involves centralising information and making it actionable for decision-making.
- **Employer of Choice / Talent Retention**: Focus on creating a positive work environment, competitive salaries, stability, and growth opportunities to retain key talent.

### V. Estructura Organizacional (Organisational Structure)

talisis operates as a **fractal organism**, meaning its principles replicate at every level.

- **Hierarchical Levels**:
    - **Corporative talisis**: Acts as the central integrating and optimising platform. It sets the strategic vision and aligns all entities.
    - **Educational Brands**: Includes UNID, U-ERRE, Harmon Hall, Advenio, and IESalud. Each brand preserves its unique identity and strengths.
    - **Campuses/Centres**: With 190 learning points, these respond to local needs.
    - **Classrooms/Experiences**: Representing over 150,000 daily interactions.
- **Principles of Replication**:
    - **talisis Way Culture**: This culture is expected to permeate and be lived at all organisational levels.
    - **Centralised Processes, Localised Experiences**: While core processes might be centralised for efficiency, the student experience is adapted to local contexts.
    - **Unified Technology, Adaptive Pedagogy**: Technology is unified across the organisation, but teaching methods are flexible and responsive to diverse learning needs.
- **Inter-area Collaboration**:
    - **Orchestrated Collaboration**: Ideally, collaboration is seen as an "orchestra" where different areas, despite their individual roles, contribute to a unified outcome.
    - **Communication as Key**: Clear, assertive, and continuous communication is highlighted as fundamental for effective collaboration.
    - **Cross-Functional Teams**: There's a vision for more multidisciplinary teams and direct interaction to break down silos and improve results.
    - **Shared Objectives**: The implementation of OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) aims to align all areas towards common, measurable goals.
    - **Bridge-Building Roles**: Some areas, like HRBP and Marketing, see their role as connecting different departments, channeling needs, and fostering cocreation.
    - **Operational Integration**: Operations is described as an integrating area for all projects and initiatives, ensuring they are effectively delivered to the student level.
- **Decision-Making**:
    - **Data-Driven**: Decisions are increasingly expected to be based on data and insights, moving away from intuition.
    - **Committees and Governance**: Key decisions for strategic projects are intended to be made through technical and governance committees, ensuring transparency and agility.
    - **Leadership Role**: Leaders are expected to provide clear vision, guidance, and support, empowering their teams while maintaining strategic alignment.

### VI. Resultado Financiero del Negocio (Financial Business Results)

Financial health is a critical aspect of talisis's strategic vision, deeply integrated into its planning and operational decisions.

- **Core Financial Mission**: The Administration and Finance department's mission is to ensure the **accuracy of accounting and financial operations** for decision-making, lead budgetary planning, analyse key indicators for efficiency and profitability, comply with fiscal regulations, and proactively manage risks with robust internal controls.
- **Financial Health Goals**: Alberto Sánchez, Director of Planning, highlights the need for a **healthy income statement and balance sheet**, with sufficient cash flow, to support all internal and external needs, including CAPEX, and achieve long-term objectives. Marcela Valdez, Director of Administration and Finance, underlines the importance of a **responsible and ethical financial approach** that optimises scarce resources to enable student growth and transform lives.
- **Key Financial Indicators and Metrics**:
    - **Profitability and Margins**: Focus on "rentabilidad" (profitability), "margen de contribución" (contribution margin), and "margen operativo" (operating margin). The goal is to ensure good growth in revenues and profitability of brands and the company.
    - **Cash Flow and Debt Management**: Efficient administration of debt and capital raising is crucial. Maintaining a minimum cash flow is a short-term priority, especially in challenging economic environments.
    - **Cost of Acquisition (CAC/CPL)**: A key metric for marketing and sales areas to measure efficiency in acquiring new students. The aim is to reduce it while maintaining quality.
    - **Return on Investment (ROI)**: Expected ROI for technological investments and ROI of campaigns.
    - **Productivity and Efficiency**: Tracking "productividad incremental" (incremental productivity), "eficiencia operativa" (operational efficiency), and optimising cost and expense allocation.
    - **Debt-to-Equity Ratio, Interest-to-EBITDA Ratio, EBITDA-to-Debt Ratio**: Specific financial ratios mentioned for strategic projects like Promecap's exit strategy.
    - **Revenue Growth**: Measured by new enrolments, income forecasts, and increases in the number of students.
- **Financial Challenges**:
    - **Complicated Economic Environment**: A "complicated and recessive economic environment" poses a significant challenge, impacting payment collection and requiring careful financial management to maintain minimum profitability.
    - **Portfolio Recovery**: A primary short-term focus is the recovery of the outstanding portfolio (accounts receivable).
    - **Data Accuracy and Timeliness**: Obtaining financial information with sufficient "oportunidad" (timeliness) and accuracy is a challenge, as is the lack of proper master data design and periodic closures in core systems (like Banner vs. SAP), leading to discrepancies and risks.
    - **Project Prioritisation**: The need to prioritise projects that ensure short-term financial stability, even if they delay potentially more prosperous long-term initiatives.
- **Financial Role as Enabler**: Marcela Valdez sees her area as an "enabler" from the back office, ensuring administrative and financial processes support the growth and transformation driven by commercial and operational areas. This includes accompanying invoicing, managing expenses, and allocating resources and investments to new products and formats.

### VII. Procesos y Sistema de Gestión (Processes and Management System)

talisis places a significant emphasis on formalising and optimising its processes and management systems, recognising their crucial role in achieving strategic objectives and ensuring operational efficiency.

- **Process Formalisation and Documentation**:
    - A major challenge identified is that "there are certain processes that are not documented". Alberto Sánchez's team is actively working on documenting these to ensure compliance once authorised by leaders.
    - Alejandro Madrazo, Academic Director, stresses that ideal collaboration requires "very clear and very predictable processes" to allow for structured and efficient planning, avoiding constant emergency interventions.
    - Marcos Samaniego, Academic Operations Director, points out the need for a "document repository of all processes" due to the company's size and complexity, to centralise information and ensure consistent communication across all areas.
- **Quality Management System (QMS)**:
    - Inge Ploennig, Director of Quality and Academic Accreditation, spearheads the consolidation of a robust QMS. This system is designed to function naturally within daily operations, independent of external accreditation cycles, reducing stress and rework.
    - The QMS integrates with existing frameworks like the "talisis Way" (13 strategic axes) and cascaded objectives from the institutional mission.
    - It also focuses on formalising and strengthening "rituals" (regular meetings and follow-ups) that bring order and clarity to processes.
- **Data-Driven Decision Making**:
    - There's a strong drive to use data for informed decision-making across all departments.
    - This requires improving data quality, ensuring timely access, and integrating disparate data sources and platforms.
    - Luis Ruiz, Software Development Manager, highlights the priority of building a comprehensive "data architecture" to collect and centralise information from students, teachers, and administrators for better understanding and solution generation.
    - Marcos Samaniego is implementing centralised dashboards and automated metrics for key operational processes.
- **Performance Measurement and OKRs/KPIs**:
    - The implementation and refinement of an OKR (Objectives and Key Results) system are ongoing. This aims to provide clear, measurable objectives across the organisation, fostering continuous feedback.
    - KPIs (Key Performance Indicators) are used to track various aspects, from financial health (EBITDA, debt ratios) to operational efficiency (cost per lead, conversion rates) and student/employee satisfaction (NPS, retention, engagement).
    - There's an emphasis on building historical data sets for deeper analysis and trend identification.
- **Digitalisation and Automation**:
    - A significant push to automate manual processes using AI and cloud technology, such as financial reconciliations, compensation calculations, and student onboarding processes.
    - This automation is expected to free up human time for higher-value, strategic activities.
- **Onboarding Processes**: Marcela Valdez highlights the need to design smoother onboarding processes for students, employees, and suppliers, streamlining administrative transitions.
- **Academic Accreditations**: Maintaining and pursuing academic accreditations (like FINPES, TOEFL, TOEIC) is a continuous process that validates quality and adds market value, with a current system-level reaccreditation underway for UNID.

### VIII. Transformación y Gestión del Cambio (Transformation and Change Management)

Transformation and change management are central to talisis's strategy, aiming to move towards a more agile, technologically advanced, and human-centred educational ecosystem.

- **Designing and Sustaining Change**: Julián Mendez, Gerente de Planeación Operativa y Transformación, states that his area's mission is not just to operate change, but to **design and activate it, sustaining it until it becomes part of the culture**. This involves working on processes, capabilities, technology, and, crucially, culture.
- **Catalyst for Digital Transformation**: Technology is viewed as a "catalyst of digital transformation," particularly AI, which is expected to "super-humanise" employees rather than replace them, by automating routine tasks and freeing up time for strategic thinking and human interaction.
- **Cultural Adoption**: A significant challenge is ensuring that all members of the organisation embrace and internalise the changes. This includes overcoming "resistance to change" and a "lack of digital mindset". Mariel Serrano emphasises that **reputation starts from within**, requiring every collaborator to feel proud and committed to talisis Way.
- **Strategic vs. Operational Focus**: Leaders like Alberto Sánchez and Marcela Valdez acknowledge the tension between urgent short-term operational demands and the need for long-term strategic transformation. The aim is to balance these by focusing on high-impact projects and adapting processes to current operational needs.
- **Agility and Experimentation**: There's an emphasis on having an "agile mindset" and being open to experimentation. Carlos Villa and Román highlight the benefits of breaking projects into small phases to allow for quick adjustments and continuous advancement.
- **Leadership Role in Change**: Leaders are expected to drive the transformation by:
    - **Communicating Clear Vision**: Ensuring that the strategic vision is clear and consistently communicated across all levels of the organisation.
    - **Empowering Teams**: Giving teams autonomy and supporting their development to foster innovation.
    - **Leading by Example**: Demonstrating commitment and positive attitude towards the changes.
    - **Fostering Collaboration**: Breaking down silos and promoting cross-functional work to ensure integrated efforts.
- **Continuous Improvement**: The philosophy of "mejora continua" (continuous improvement) is embedded in the approach, with regular measurement and feedback loops to refine processes and strategies.
- **Adaptation to External Context**: The organisation is continuously monitoring external trends (e.g., technology, market, socio-political) to adapt its strategies and offerings. The goal is to be proactive and anticipate future needs rather than just reacting.

talisis’s growth strategy is multifaceted, combining internal optimisation with external expansion, all while focusing on sustainable and quality-driven growth.

- **Growth Objectives**: The overall objective is to expand reach and accessibility, becoming one of the largest educational groups nationally and potentially internationally by 2035. This means growing in terms of student numbers, new brands, and market share.
- **Organic Growth**:

    - **New Product Development**: Continuously develop new academic programmes and offerings aligned with market needs and future trends. Álvaro García mentions that new content and learning resources will require official validity recognition, indicating proactive regulatory engagement for new products.
    - **Portfolio Utilisation**: Strategically use talisis's rich portfolio of brands and products to target diverse market segments and communities.
- **Inorganic Growth (M&A and Expansion)**:
    - **Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A)**: Alberto Sánchez indicates that M&A opportunities are a key long-term project to consolidate the business. Financial resources (capital or debt raising) are a primary challenge for this.
    - **Geographic Expansion**: Explore expanding the presence of existing brands (e.g., UR regionally). This also includes opening new learning centres or "hubs" in strategic cities, potentially with more financially viable models (e.g., hybrid models, franchises) than full campuses.

- **Capacity Management Challenges**:
    - **Operational Control**: Before significant expansion, there's a need to have existing operations "sumamente controlado" (highly controlled).
    - **Talent Capacity**: Ensuring sufficient, adequately skilled personnel to manage growth and innovation projects is a recurring challenge.
    - **Technology as Scaler**: Technology is seen as essential for achieving scale and efficiency, especially with AI, to automate processes and enable a wider reach with fewer human hands.
- **Talent Development for Growth**:
    - **Professional Development**: Continuous professional development for teams is prioritised to ensure they have the skills needed for future challenges and technologies.
    - **Leadership Development**: Developing leaders who can drive high-performance and manage transformation is critical for scaling.
    - **Succession Planning**: Developing key personnel to become "cartas de reemplazo" (replacement cards) for executives is a long-term HR priority.
- **Efficiency and Optimisation**:
    - **Cost Efficiency**: Efforts to optimise the efficient distribution of costs and expenses using technology, rather than just reducing them, are in place.
    - **Productivity Improvement**: Projects target improving commercial productivity through automation and better visibility.
- **Sustainability**: The concept of "sustainability" is integrated into processes to ensure growth is not just quantitative but also purposeful and long-lasting.
