### **Slide 3: Core Challenge – Governance for Effective Execution**

**The Question of Governance**

- How can Tec best structure governance to effectively and sustainably achieve this ambitious LLL strategy?

**Key Governance Objectives**

- **Prioritization:** Focus on high-impact segments within B2B and B2C markets.
- **Efficiency:** Streamline decision-making and resource allocation across campuses.
- **Agility:** Enable rapid response to emerging technologies and shifting learner needs.

**Approach**

- An integrated governance model that aligns decision-making, accountability, and support functions across the institution.

---

### **Slide 4: Governance Structure – Proposed Model**

**1. Strategic Leadership Team (SLT)**

- **Role:** Establish the vision, approve strategic priorities, allocate resources.
- **Members:** Core executives including the President, VP for Future of Learning, VP for Administration and Finance, and VP for Academic Affairs.
- **Responsibilities:** Ensure alignment with Tec’s overall strategic vision, set annual goals, and approve large-scale initiatives.

**2. Core Execution Team (CET)**

- **Role:** Implement strategic directives, oversee daily execution.
- **Members:** Key leaders within the Lifelong Learning unit, including:
    - **Víctor Gutiérrez** – VP of Learning for the Future, strategy and vision alignment​.
    - **Sandra Castañeda** – Director of Administration and Finance, oversees operational execution​.
    - **Elsa Fernández** – Director of Experience and Digital Ecosystems, responsible for digital transformation and user experience​.
    - **Fermín Legorreta** – Talent Director, leads talent strategy and capacity building​.
    - **Jorge Blando** – Director of Continuing Education, drives curriculum and program development​.
    - **Dr. Gaspar Fuentes** – Director of Strategic Initiatives, steers campus model development​.
- **Responsibilities:** Directly manage implementation, drive team accountability, and execute day-to-day governance processes.

---

### **Slide 5: Strategic Governance – Key Processes and Workflows**

**1. Decision-Making Process**

- **Framework:** Decisions are made based on a dual focus of strategic alignment with Tec’s core priorities and market-driven insights.
- **Monthly Review:** CET meets monthly to review KPIs, market changes, and technology shifts to ensure agile responsiveness.

**2. Resource Allocation Workflow**

- **Prioritization of Segments:** The CET will prioritize segments with the highest growth potential in the B2B and B2C markets based on detailed segmentation data​.
- **Budget Reviews:** Quarterly budget re-evaluations led by Sandra Castañeda, with a focus on maximizing ROI and aligning with the institution's goals.

**3. Cross-functional Collaboration**

- **Inter-departmental Teams:** Specialized task forces (e.g., Digital Transformation, Market Research) reporting to CET, ensuring aligned objectives across functions like Marketing, IT, and Academic Affairs.
- **Accountability:** Quarterly strategy progress meetings with the SLT to report milestones, address bottlenecks, and adjust course if necessary.

---

### **Slide 6: Monitoring and Measuring Impact**

**1. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**

- **Market Penetration:** Growth in B2B and B2C segment share, including total learners served.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Assessment of learner satisfaction, career impact, and learning transfer.
- **Efficiency Metrics:** Cost per learner, time to deploy new offerings, and technological adoption rates.

**2. Quarterly Review Cycle**

- **Data-Driven Adjustments:** The CET will conduct quarterly assessments of KPIs and market feedback, implementing course corrections as needed to stay aligned with strategic priorities.
- **Stakeholder Reporting:** Key insights and results will be reported to SLT and stakeholders to maintain transparency and trust.

---

### **Slide 7: Enabling Digital and Technological Transformation**

**Harnessing Technology for Lifelong Learning**

- Implement AI and learning analytics across programs to enhance learner personalization.

**Technology Governance**

### **Slide 8: Risk Management and Contingency Planning**

**Identified Risks**

- **Market Competition:** Rapid shifts in the competitive landscape, especially from tech-based educational startups.
- **Operational Complexity:** Coordinating across multiple campuses with diverse learner demographics.
- **Financial Sustainability:** Ensuring ROI while expanding market reach and maintaining educational quality.

**Mitigation Strategies**

- **Competitive Analysis:** Continuous assessment of market trends led by Dr. Gaspar Fuentes.
- **Operational Efficiency:** Establishing streamlined workflows across campuses to mitigate complexity.
- **Financial Projections and Flexibility:** Bi-annual financial reviews led by Sandra Castañeda to ensure agile resource management.
