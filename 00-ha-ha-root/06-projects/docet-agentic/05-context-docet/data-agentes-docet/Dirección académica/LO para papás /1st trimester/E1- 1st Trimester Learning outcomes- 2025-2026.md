School year 2025-2026
Instituto Docet

Trimester learning outcomes

Level: Elementary

Grade: First Grade

1st Trimester
Subject

Learning outcomes
Expresión oral:
●​ Expresa verbalmente sus ideas y pensamientos.
●​ Comprende claramente las instrucciones.
●​ Realiza comentarios de los textos ilustrados.

Español

Escritura:
●​ Escribe su nombre completo con letra clara y correcta.​
Redacta ideas propias mediante escritura espontánea.
●​ Traza letras con direccionalidad y tamaño adecuado.
●​ Distingue mayúsculas y minúsculas en forma y uso.
●​ Respeta el espacio entre palabras y el uso del renglón.
●​ Crea rimas sencillas a partir de modelos conocidos.
Lectura:
●​ Comprende el contenido de textos sencillos.
●​ Lee sílabas con vocales y consonantes variantes y complejas.
●​ Sigue el programa de lectura que mejora la comprensión, la fluidez y el pensamiento crítico.

Reflexión sobre la lengua:
Gramática:
●​
●​
Ortografía:
●​
●​

Identifica y usa el sustantivo propio y común.
Identifica y utiliza adecuadamente los artículos determinados e indeterminados
Usa la mayúscula en los sustantivos (nombre, lugar) y para indicar el inicio de párrafos.
Comprende y aplica adecuadamente el punto final en una oración.

Vocabulario:
●​ Incrementa su vocabulario integrando palabras nuevas de proyectos y lecturas.

Reading:
●​ Reads simple decodable books using picture support.
●​ Identifies characters, settings, and major events in a story.
●​ Matches spoken words to printed words during shared reading.
●​ Follows the reading program that enhances comprehension, fluency and critical thinking.
Phonics:

English

●​ Identifies and produces initial, medial, and final sounds in CVC words.
●​ Blends simple sounds to read short words.
●​ Recognizes rhyming words and beginning sounds.
Writing:
●​
●​
●​
●​

Writes simple sentences with correct word order, starting with a capital letter and ending with a period.
Participates in shared writing using sentence starters.
Labels pictures with single words or short phrases.
Recognizes and uses basic punctuation marks such as periods in simple sentences.

Grammar:
●​
●​
●​
●​

Recognizes and uses common nouns in oral sentences.
Identifies adjectives to describe objects.
Identifies and uses the verb to be in a sentence.
Recognizes and reads basic sight words in isolation and simple sentences.

Speaking/Listening:
●​ Listens actively and responds to simple questions.
●​ Participates in group discussions by taking turns.
●​ Describes pictures or personal experiences using short sentences.

Conceptos Numéricos
Matemáticas

●​ Identifica, escribe, compara y ordena los números naturales del 0 al 20.
●​ Comprende el concepto del cero y cuenta de dos en dos, de cinco en cinco y de diez en diez hasta el 20.
●​ Práctica cálculo mental para la suma y la resta.

Numbers Concepts:
●​ Identifies, writes, compares, and orders natural numbers from 0 to 20.
●​ Understands the concept of zero and counts by 2s, 5s, and 10s up to 20.
Math
Addition and subtraction
●​ Practices mental math for addition and subtraction.
●​ Solves addition and subtraction sentences using number lines and mental strategies.

●​ Evaluates the correctness of addition and subtraction operations.
Graphing and data Interpretation
●​
●​
●​
●​

Understands what data is and how it becomes information and knowledge.
Collects and represents data using graphs, images, drawings, tables, and creative formats.
Interprets and organizes data into up to three categories.
Uses maps and simple Cartesian coordinates to represent direction and spatial information (left, right, up,
down).

Geometry:
●​
●​
●​
●​
●​

Recognizes and classifies open and closed 2D figures (circle, square, triangle, rectangle, etc.).
Understands symmetry, regularity, and distinguishes between 2D and 3D shapes.
Identifies and names 3D figures (rectangular prisms, cubes), counting vertices, edges, and faces.
Understands shape movements: slides, flips, and turns; distinguishes symmetry and asymmetry.
Follows and gives directions using a coordinate plane.

Mindfulness
●​ Recognizes and names emotions, bodily sensations, and behaviors when they are calm or agitated.
●​ Identifies moments when they are able to maintain attention and focus.
●​ Uses their senses to appreciate and value aspects of their environment.
Lab for life
Self-Control
●​ Identifies situations that trigger difficult emotions (anger, frustration, fear, sadness).
●​ Recognizes the importance of self-control and practices simple techniques to manage impulses with adult
support.

●​ Values the positive effects of maintaining self-control on themselves and others.
Self-Motivation
●​ Recognizes their personal characteristics and abilities as tools to achieve goals and fulfill responsibilities.
●​ Identifies what they are already able to do on their own and what they want to improve in the short term.
●​ Whit support, plan actions and overcome obstacles to reach a specific goal.

Science lab

Human body:
●​ Identifies and describes the external parts of the human body and explains their basic functions.
Living and non-living things:
●​ Observes and compares living and non-living things, identifying what living things need to survive. Then,
observes and compares plants and animals, identifying visible features and their functions.
Life cycles:
●​ Observes and describes the life cycles of animals, identifying behaviors between parents and their young
that help the young survive at each stage.
Digital citizenship:
●​ Defines what digital citizenship is.
●​ Understands that the information he/she shares online leaves a digital footprint.

Digital Planet lab
Digital tools:
●​ Practices with the mouse, opening and closing windows and turning on and off the computer.
●​ Gets familiar with the keyboard and the position of the keys: j, f, space and u.
Programming:
●​ Understands the concept of algorithm and programming and translates a list of steps into a series of physical
actions in coding programs.

Design and Fabrication:
Robotics lab

●​ Identifies and names common 2D shapes such as circles, squares, triangles, and rectangles.
●​ Uses 2D software like Pixlart for basic digital art and design.
Electronics:
●​ Understands basic principles of electricity and circuits.
Programming:
●​ Learns block-based programming using Lego Spike.

Art lab

●​ Demonstrates an understanding of line and shape by creating a pencil drawing with the application of light
and shadow.
●​ Applies knowledge of color theory and color mixing to create a landscape composition with gradients.
●​ Demonstrates understanding of watercolor painting techniques, including transparency and fluidity, and
incorporates applied or visual textures into the artwork.
Stone age art:
●​ Creates a three-dimensional sculpture using clay, inspired by Stone Age art and historical context.

Music lab

●​ Recognizes and represents basic rhythmic values visually and through body movement: quarter note, half
note, eighth note, sixteenth note, and whole note, applying them in simple percussion and movement
exercises.
●​ Sings and performs simple fragments of the Do Major and La minor scales using Kodály hand signs,
distinguishing dynamics mezzoforte and forte.
●​ Identifies and names basic notes on the treble clef staff, focusing on the notes of the Do Major and La minor
scales.

Dance

●​ Recognises basic spatial and bodily concepts such as left, right, down, up and coordinates the body
accordingly.
●​ Memorises and performs a jazz choreographical piece.
●​ Recognises and names the parts of a theatre.

Deportes

Equilibrio:
●​ Mantiene el equilibrio en actividades sencillas, como caminar en línea recta, saltar con ambos pies y pararse
en una pierna.
●​ Comienza a hacer movimientos más difíciles, como caminar sobre una cuerda o viga de equilibrio con
estabilidad.
Reacción:
●​ Responde rápido a un estímulo visual o de sonido.
●​ Sigue instrucciones simples y reacciona a señales en juegos, aunque todavía necesita tiempo para
instrucciones más difíciles.
Ritmo:
●​ Sigue ritmos básicos con palmas, pasos o movimientos simples.
●​ Mantiene un ritmo en actividades como cantar y moverse siguiendo una secuencia.
Juegos predeportivos:
●​ Fútbol, atletismo y béisbol: Es capaz de participar activamente en juegos predeportivos y recreativos,
demostrando habilidades motoras básicas, así como coordinación y control corporal.
