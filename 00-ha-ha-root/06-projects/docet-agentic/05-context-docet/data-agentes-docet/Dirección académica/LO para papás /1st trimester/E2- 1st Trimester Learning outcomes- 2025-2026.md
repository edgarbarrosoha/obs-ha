School year 2025-2026
Instituto Docet

Trimester learning outcomes

Level: Elementary

Grade: Second Grade

1st Trimester
Subject

Learning outcomes
Expresión oral:
●​ Dialoga sobre anécdotas familiares, personales y del entorno inmediato.
●​ Expone ante el grupo temas previamente acordados.
Lectura:
●​ Lee y comprende instrucciones escritas.

Español

●​ Realiza Lecturas con mayor fluidez, comprendiendo su significado y cuidando su tono de voz y
entonación.
●​ Practica la fluidez y comprensión lectora utilizando la plataforma de Raz Kids y programa de lectura.
●​ Sigue el programa de lectura que mejora la comprensión, la fluidez y el pensamiento crítico.
Escritura:
●​ Escribe oraciones incluyendo las partes de la oración (sujeto y predicado).
●​ Escribe descripciones de objetos, personas, lugares e ilustraciones utilizando detalles y adjetivos para
enriquecer sus escritos.

Reflexión sobre la lengua:
Gramática
●​ Utiliza sustantivos comunes y propios.
●​ Utiliza adecuadamente los artículos determinados en sus oraciones. (el, la, los, las).
●​ Utiliza adecuadamente los artículos indeterminados. (un, uno, una, unas).
●​ Identifica en oraciones diversas a la persona que realiza la acción.
Ortografía:
●​ Utiliza mayúsculas en nombres propios, después de punto y al principio de la oración.
●​ Escribe respetando el espacio que separa las palabras.
●​ Identifica el renglón donde llevará a cabo su escritura.
Vocabulario
●​ Reconoce la definición de un campo semántico y lo representa con objetos del entorno.

English

Reading
●​ Decodes regularly spelled two-syllable words with short vowels.
●​ Knows and applies grade-level phonics and word analysis skills in decoding words.
●​ Identifies fiction and nonfiction texts.
●​ Reads grade-level text with purpose and understanding.
●​ Follows the reading program that enhances comprehension, fluency and critical thinking.

Phonics
●​ Demonstrates command of simple conventions and usage when writing or speaking.
●​ Asks and answers questions using: who, what, where, when, why, and how to demonstrate understanding.

Writing
●​ Capitalizes names, holidays, product names, and geographic names.
Grammar
●​ Identifies the subject and predicate in a sentence.
●​ Uses common and proper nouns.
●​ Uses regular and irregular and collective nouns.
●​ Uses singular and plural nouns.
●​ Identifies and uses regular verbs in present and past.
●​ Identifies and uses adjectives.
●​ Writes personal and possessive pronouns.
Speaking and listening
●​ Participates in collaborative conversations with diverse partners.
●​ Tells a story or recounts an experience with appropriate facts and descriptive details.
Numbers
●​ Learns and order different ways to express a number from 0 to 100.
●​ Learns and uses a number line and number chart.
●​ Identifies and names place value of hundreds, tens and ones.

Math

Addition
●​ Models additions that equal 10 and adds 10 to any number.
●​ Uses a number line to solve additions.
●​ Distinguishes the application of doubles to solve additions.
●​ Uses a ten value to solve additions.
●​ Works with column additions and solves additions of three numbers.
Substraction
●​ Learns the result when subtracting the same number or zero.
●​ Subtracts using a number line.
●​ Subtracts by counting back.
●​ Forms fact families to ease the finding of a result.

Graphing
●​ Learns how to run a survey and graph and how to interpret it.
●​ Learns how to plot and read a numbered grid.
Numbers (0-100)
●​ Learns, compares and order different ways to express a number from 0 to 100.
●​ Uses a number line and a number chart.
Addition I (0-99)
●​ Recognizes the answer of a problem when using the value 10.
●​ Understands when and how to regroup.
Subtraction II (0-99)
●​ Understands the action of subtract using models and charts.
●​ Understands when and how to regroup.
Geometry
●​ Recognizes and names each solid shape.
●​ Distinguishes sides,corners and angles on a plane shape figure.
●​ Recognizes and models quadrilateral and ttri-dimensional objects.
●​ Understands how to get perimeter values.
Mindfulness
Lab for life

●​ Identifies the qualities that help them maintain attention and concentration.
●​ Recognizes that thoughts trigger emotions and describe their bodily sensations.
●​ Differentiates the effects of calmness and restlessness.
Self-Control
●​ Names situations that trigger difficult emotions and identify the thoughts associated with them.
●​ Recognizes impulsive behaviors and practices regulation techniques.​

●​ Expresses how they feel after managing their impulses and identifies areas where they need to improve.​
Self-Motivation
●​ Identifies activities they want to accomplish as short-term goals.
●​ Names the actions needed to achieve a goal and recognize their importance.
●​ Recognizes obstacles and the emotions associated with the process, with adult support.​​

​

Science lab

Plants and animals:
●​ Identifies and describes the main external parts of plants and animals and explains how they help them
meet their basic needs.
●​ Observes and compares living organisms in different habitats, focusing on features like texture, body
parts, and how they move, eat, or grow.
●​ Describes how plants and animals interact with natural components like water, air, sunlight, and soil in their
environments.

Robotics lab

Fabrication and Design:
●​ Understands safety procedures for cutting and assembling materials with precision using scissors and
cardboard cutters.
●​ Accurately measures and interprets lengths and dimensions using units (cm and mm) and understands
technical drawings, demonstrating proficiency in measurement and scale.
Electronics:
●​ Identifies basic electronic components and designs and simulates electronic circuits using Tinkercad
software.
Programming:
●​ Understands block programming concepts and terminology, and programs robots to perform simple tasks
using lego spike software.

Digital Planet lab

Digital Citizenship:
●​ Develops a comprehensive understanding of online account safety, from identifying what an online
account is and why it needs to be protected, to applying account safety rules to create a memorable and
strong password.
Programming:
●​ Translates movements into a series of commands and orders them in a program, using loops and
conditionals to control actions and move in different directions. (Code.org level C).
●​ Learns the basic concepts of Scratch Jr, including character management, scenes, and wait times. (Scratch
Jr.).
Digital Tools:
●​ Gets familiar with keys: T, S, L, O, B, A.
●​ Understands how to open and close his/her session on gmail.

Music

●​ Recognizes and plays basic rhythms with quarter, half, eighth, sixteenth, and whole notes in 2/4 and 4/4
time, using the body and percussion instruments.
●​ Sings and listens to seconds and thirds, using Kodály hand signs and simple songs with notes from Do
Major and La minor.
●​ Uses loud and soft sounds (piano, mezzoforte, forte) and different ways of singing or playing (legato and
staccato) in music activities.​

Dance

●​ Recognises and comprehends what corporal laterality is and coordinates the body accordingly, following
music's rhythm.
●​ Memorises and performs a jazz/contemporary choreographical piece.
●​ Recognises parts of a theatre and basic theatre lighting concepts.​

Deportes

Equilibrio:
●​ Mantiene el equilibrio en superficies variadas y complejas, demostrando estabilidad al realizar
movimientos dinámicos (saltos y giros) y estáticos, y al desplazarse sobre vigas o líneas.
Reacción:

●​ Responde de manera rápida y precisa a estímulos sensoriales (visuales y auditivos), adaptándose
efectivamente a situaciones cambiantes y siguiendo instrucciones complejas en juegos y actividades
físicas.
Ritmo:
●​ Sigue y ajusta ritmos complejos, coordinando sus movimientos corporales con patrones rítmicos en juegos
y actividades físicas con o sin implementos.
Juegos predeportivos
●​ Es capaz de participar en juegos predeportivos y recreativos (fútbol, atletismo, balonmano) consolidando
y perfeccionando habilidades motoras básicas, demostrando una mayor coordinación y control corporal.
●​ Fortalece su actitud positiva hacia la actividad física y el trabajo en equipo, mientras aplica y refuerza
conceptos simples de cooperación, respeto por las reglas y fair play, todo en un entorno lúdico y
motivador.
Art

Art techniques:
●​ Demonstrates an understanding of watercolor painting techniques and drawing fundamentals by creating
a botanical illustration with both transparent washes and precise line work.
●​ Creates a composition that effectively uses contrast and a gradient background, applying knowledge of
color theory to create emphasis.
Ancient Greece:
●​ Creates a design for a vase that reflects the historical and contextual characteristics of Ancient Greece,
specifically drawing on the main characteristics of Greek pottery.
Roman art:
●​ Applies the trecandís technique (mosaic) to create a mural design that shows an understanding of Roman
art and composition.
