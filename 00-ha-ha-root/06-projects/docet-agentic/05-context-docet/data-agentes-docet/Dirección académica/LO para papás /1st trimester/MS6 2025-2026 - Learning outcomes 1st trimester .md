Learning outcomes

Español
●​ Expresa con sus palabras las ideas que comprende de los textos que lee, incorporando nuevas palabras
en su vocabulario.
●​ Lee textos biográficos e identifica las relaciones temporales de secuencia, simultaneidad y duración
(nexos).
●​ Combina textos literarios con elementos de otros lenguajes artísticos, donde establezca movimientos,
gestos, sonidos o colores para representar personajes, lugares y situaciones para su apreciación y
disfrute.
●​ Escribe y reflexiona sobre la importancia de las manifestaciones culturales y artísticas conocidas en el
mundo.
●​ Escribe diferentes tipos de reseñas, considerando sus características
●​ Elabora resúmenes que le permitan reconstruir las ideas principales y los elementos de los textos
explicativos, haciendo uso adecuado de la ortografía y gramática de la lengua.
●​ Sigue el programa de lectura que mejora la comprensión, la fluidez y el pensamiento crítico.
Historia

●​ Identifica la duración del periodo y la secuencia del origen del ser humano, del poblamiento de los
continentes y de la sedentarización aplicando términos como siglo, milenio y a.C.
●​ Ubica espacialmente el origen del ser humano, el poblamiento de los continentes y los lugares donde
se domesticaron las primeras plantas y animales
●​ Explica la evolución del ser humano y la relación con la naturaleza durante la prehistoria.
●​ Compara las actividades y las formas de vida nómada y sedentaria
●​ Reconoce la importancia de la invención de la escritura y las características de las primeras ciudades.

English

●​ Identifies and uses basic sentence elements: The student can identify and use nouns, pronouns, and
verbs to construct simple sentences.
●​ Applies active and passive voice: The student can use the active and passive voice correctly and can
rewrite sentences by changing the voice from one to the other.
●​ Understands and uses modal verbs: The student can identify modal verbs, understand their function,
and use them accurately to convey a specific meaning.
●​ Demonstrates listening and reading comprehension: The student can understand short conversations
and written texts, and answer questions about them.

Social Studies

●​ Applies chronological conventions (B.C.E./C.E. or B.C./A.D.) to arrange and analyze historical events.
●​ Explains how geography influenced the development of unique early civilizations like Mesopotamia and
Egypt, and describes their distinct governments, cultures, economic systems, technologies, and
agricultural practices.
●​ Utilizes maps, globes, and other geographic tools to gather information, and classifies and compares
regions within the Eastern Hemisphere based on various criteria such as landform, climate, population,
and cultural and economic characteristics.
●​ Describes the relationship between those in power and individual citizens in different government types
(democracy, dictatorship, monarchy, theocracy), and understands that these categories can overlap or
misrepresent actual government functions.

Digital Planet

Digital Citizenship
●​ Defines the fundamental concepts of artificial intelligence, including the role of data as a building block,
and analyzes the unique characteristics, applications, benefits, and drawbacks of generative AI.
AI Digital tools
●​ Stars exploring the AI chatbot Gemini and learns how to create effective prompt engineering.
Programming and App development
●​ Creates and styles web page structures using HTML for content and CSS for design.

Lab for life

Mindfulness
●​ Analyzes the internal and external factors that influence their well-being, and proposes strategies to
stay focused and maintain calmness.
●​ Recognizes and classify their emotions, evaluate their behavior when experiencing them, and
understand that they can choose how to respond in difficult situations.
●​ Practices using their senses to stay present and respond with serenity in situations of stress or conflict.
Self-control
●​ Anticipates stimuli that trigger distressing emotions and applies preventive self-control strategies
●​ Evokes positive emotions to counteract discomfort and restore balance.
Self-motivation
●​ Analyzes situations of disinterest or laziness, valuing how effort, perseverance, and motivation help
them overcome these feelings.
●​ Recognizes the characteristics of a proactive person and takes on responsibilities to achieve personal
and group goals.

Science lab

Earth and space science:
●​ Describe the main characteristics of galaxies, stars, and the solar system, identifying our location in the
universe.
●​ Explain how the force of gravity affects the motion of objects on Earth and in space.
●​ Investigate the relationship between force, mass, and acceleration, applying Newton’s Laws of Motion.

P.E.

Capacidades coordinativas y físicas
●​ Mantiene el equilibrio en movimientos más complejos, como saltar, girar o sostener objetos, mostrando
un mayor control corporal. (Equilibrio)
●​ Organiza sus movimientos en el espacio con mayor seguridad, anticipando trayectorias y sincronizando
acciones como correr y atrapar un objeto en movimiento.(Espacio y tiempo)
Handball
●​ Conoce las reglas del handball y practica los fundamentos básicos.
Acampada y aventura
●​ Reconoce los elementos básicos y el propósito de la acampada.

Art

and sets, applying texture, color, and visual appeal
●​ Understands the basic principles of cinematography in film and animation, including frame rates,
camera techniques, and object manipulation.
●​ Learns the basic techniques of video and animation editing, and applies animation techniques such as
keyframe animation, armature construction, and object replacement.
Artistic Design:
●​ Develops skills in artistic design for film and animation, including sets, applying texture, color, and visual
appeal, a general theme, and the execution of the visual story.
