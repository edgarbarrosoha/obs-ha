2nd Trimester learning outcomes

2nd Trimester

Subject
Español

Learning outcomes
Expresión oral y vocabulario
●​ Prepara y participa en una mesa redonda considerando todas sus características.
Lectura
●​ Reflexiona sobre el uso de otros lenguajes, como los audiovisuales, el lenguaje persuasivo y los
recursos retóricos utilizados en las campañas escolares y oficiales.
●​ Analiza la finalidad y características de los caligramas, acrósticos y palíndromos, reconociendo las
siguientes figuras literarias: hipérbaton, antítesis, epíteto, sinécdoque, perífrasis.
●​ Elabora esquematizaciones como producto de textos leídos o investigados, haciendo uso de mapas
mentales, mapas conceptuales, diagramas de flujo o cuadros sinópticos.
Escritura
●​ Elabora una historieta con las respectivas figuras literarias visuales, a partir de un texto narrativo leído
previamente.
●​ Crea un texto argumentativo para la mesa redonda, incluyendo correctamente las diferentes categorías
gramaticales.
Reflexión de la lengua, gramática y ortografía
●​ Aplica consistentemente las reglas ortográficas de acentuación, puntuación, uso de mayúsculas y
grafías correctas en las producciones escritas, demostrando claridad y coherencia en los textos creados.
●​ Reconoce y diferencia los tipos de nexos coordinantes y subordinantes.
●​ Pronombres: Clasificación (personales, posesivos, demostrativos) y su función como sustitutos del
sustantivo.
●​ Adverbio: Tipos (tiempo, lugar, modo, cantidad) y su función como modificador.
●​ Realiza la división silábica y clasifica correctamente las palabras como agudas, graves, esdrújulas y

sobreesdrújulas.
Math

Writing linear equations
●​ Writes and graphs lines in the point-slope form
●​ Identifies, writes, and graphs lines in the standard and general forms
●​ Solves problems using linear functions and graphs
Inequalities
●​ Understands and solves simple inequalities
●​ Solves compound inequalities and represents solutions on a number line
●​ Solves and graph one-step and multi-step inequalities
●​ Understands and solves linear inequalities in two variables to graph solutions
●​ Applies inequalities to practical problems
Systems of linear equations
●​ Uses the graphic method to solve systems of linear equations
●​ Applies elimination techniques in systems of linear equations to find solutions
●​ Writes and solve systems of linear equations to find solutions to real-life applications
Quadratic expressions
●​ Evaluates quadratic expressions in any form
●​ Factors expression in the forms ax2+bx and ax2+bx+c
●​ Solves problems factoring quadratic equations
Quadratic equations
●​ Identifies quadratic equations and describes parabolas
●​ Factors quadratic expressions in the ax2+bx form and solve equations by factoring a common term
●​ Factors quadratic expressions in the ax2+bx+c form and solve equations by factoring binomials
●​ Solves quadratic equations by the graphic method
●​ Applies quadratic equations to solve real-life problems
Rations, proportions, and percents
●​ Identifies and solves problems with inverse variation
●​ Finds and uses ratios and proportions to solve problems
●​ Calculates the percent of increase and decrease to find solutions to real-life applications
●​ Calculates simple and compound interests

English

Prepositions, Conjunctions, and Interjections
●​ Uses prepositions to structure more complex sentences.

●​ Outlines that a preposition may show direction, time, place, location, spatial relationships, or introduces
an object.
●​ Memorizes that prepositions can act as an adjective modifying a noun, as an adverb modifying a verb,
or as a nominal when used in conjunction with the verb form to be.
●​ Enhances the ability to speak about a variety of topics.
●​ Practices reading comprehension and writing composition as preparation for the TOEFL & Cambridge
Tests.
●​ Practices listening and verbal ability as preparation for the TOEFL & Cambridge Tests.
Clauses and Complex Sentences
●​ Memorizes that a clause is a group of words that contains a subject and a verb that have a relationship.​
Recognizes that a clause conveys information about what the subject is or is doing.
●​ Recognizes that a clause can function as a complete sentence or combine with other clauses to form
compound or complex sentences.
●​ Enhances the ability to speak about a variety of topics.
●​ Practices reading comprehension and writing composition as preparation for the TOEFL & Cambridge
Tests.
●​ Practices listening and verbal ability as preparation for the TOEFL & Cambridge Tests.
Verbals
●​ Employs verbals when used to enhance sentence structure.
●​ Distinguishes when verbals in grammar appear as nouns, adjectives, and adverbs.
●​ Identifies the three types of verbals: gerunds, infinitives, and participles.
●​ Enhances the ability to speak about a variety of topics.​
Practices reading comprehension and writing composition as preparation for the TOEFL & Cambridge
Tests.
●​ Practices listening and verbal ability as preparation for the TOEFL & Cambridge Tests.
Subject-Verb Agreement
●​ Recognizes that verbs must always agree with the subject in both number (singular or plural) and person
(first, second, or third).
●​ Identifies proper and improper subject/verb agreement in their own and peer writing.
●​ Creates sentences using proper subject/verb agreement.
●​ Enhances the ability to speak about a variety of topics.
●​ Practices reading comprehension and writing composition as preparation for the TOEFL & Cambridge
Tests.
●​ Practices listening and verbal ability as preparation for the TOEFL & Cambridge Tests.

Passive Voice
●​ Recognizes the way to form a sentence using passive voice.
●​ Differentiates any sentences written in active or passive voice.
●​ Uses passive voice when there is interest in the person or object that experiences an action rather than
the person performing it.
●​ Enhances the ability to speak about a variety of topics.
●​ Practices reading comprehension and writing composition as preparation for the TOEFL & Cambridge
Tests.
●​ Practices listening and verbal ability as preparation for the TOEFL & Cambridge Tests.
Science

●​ Identifies and locate elements on the periodic table and explain the significance of their position in
terms of groups and periods.
●​ Describes the differences between ionic, metallic, and covalent bonds, and explains how these bonds
form.
●​ Writes chemical formulas and names for ionic and covalent compounds, and predicts the type of
bonding that will occur between different elements based on their positions in the periodic table.

Robotics

Manufacturing and Industrial Design:
●​ Safely uses hand and power tools for carpentry, measuring, cutting, and assembling wood accurately.
Mechanics:
●​ Identifies and explains the components of chain and gear systems.
Electronics:
●​ Fabricates and assembles printed circuit boards (PCBs), including soldering and cable management
techniques (stripping, cutting, and crimping).
Programming:
●​ Uses the command line interface to program and control the Raspberry Pi.

Social Studies

●​ Explains how religion influenced political power and cultural unity in various regions of Europe, Asia,
and Africa.
●​ Explains how interest in classical learning and religious reform contributed to increased global
interaction.
●​ Analyzes how innovations in agriculture, trade, and business impacted the economic and social
development of various medieval societies.
●​ Explains how agricultural and technological improvements transformed daily life socially and

economically.
●​ Explains how specialization leads to global trade.
●​ Explains the political, social, and economic reasons for the rise of powerful centralized nation-states and
empires.
●​ Explains the causes and effects of exploration and expansion.
●​ Analyzes colonization in terms of the desire for access to resources and markets as well as the
consequences on indigenous cultures, populations, and the environment.
●​ Analyzes how the desire for farmable land created conflict and impacted the physical environments of
Europe, Asia, Africa, and the Americas.
Digital Planet

Digital Citizenship
●​ Learns to spot the design tricks that make apps hard to put down and creates better, healthier designs
that are good for users.
●​ Understands fundamental AI concepts and their applications.
Programming and app development:
●​ Learns how to create interactive video games.

Lab for life

Self-care and Wellbeing
●​ Takes responsibility for their well-being by caring for both body and mind, and applies short-, medium-,
and long-term strategies.
●​ Recognizes their responsibility in situations of risk.​
Responsibility and Personal Commitment
●​ Takes initiative in planning and carrying out small self-directed projects to achieve personal goals.
●​ Practices responsibility and commitment by fulfilling school and household tasks.
Gratitude
●​ Identifies positive aspects of their life and expresses gratitude toward others.
●​ Builds bonds with peers and teachers to improve the social environment through collaboration.

P.E.

Capacidades coordinativas y físicas
●​ Incrementa su fuerza en ejercicios que combinan velocidad y potencia, como carreras con arranque,
saltos más largos o acciones de oposición con compañeros.(Fuerza)
●​ Incrementa su velocidad en acciones más complejas, combinando arranques, frenadas y giros con
rapidez, adaptándose a situaciones de juego.(Velocidad)

Deportes Flag
●​ Conoce las formaciones (ofensivas y defensivas) del flag, los fundamentos técnicos con mayor detalle,
las reglas oficiales con más amplitud y el equipamiento reglamentario, comprendiendo su función
dentro del juego organizado.
Tecnología en el deporte
●​ Utiliza herramientas básicas para analizar su propio rendimiento deportivo.
Music

Art

●​ Identifies, performs, and analyzes modal scales such as Dorian and Mixolydian, applying modal
harmonies in song analysis and performance.
●​ Reads, interprets, and performs music featuring multiple and changing time signatures, demonstrating
rhythmic accuracy and flexibility.
●​ Executes advanced dynamic and articulation techniques, performing pieces with expressive subtlety,
and analyzes recordings emphasizing articulation clarity.
Cinematography:
●​ Develops a compelling story, creating engaging characters, plot, and dialogue for a short film.
●​ Applies visual storytelling techniques, using camera angles, lighting, and composition to convey the
narrative.
●​ Directs and edits a short film, demonstrating expertise in pacing, timing, and visual flow.
●​ Collaborates with cast and crew, demonstrating effective communication.
