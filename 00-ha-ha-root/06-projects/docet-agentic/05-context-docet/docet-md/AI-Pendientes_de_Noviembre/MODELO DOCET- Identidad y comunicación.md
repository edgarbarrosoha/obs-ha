Foundations: Shapes, Circuits, and Block Coding
Students establish foundational knowledge by identifying 2D and 3D shapes and their measurements (H, L, W). They grasp the basic principles of electricity and circuits and learn block-based programming (Lego Spike) to control simple robot tasks and integrate sensors.

Measurement, Design Views, and Simple Machines
Students develop precision in measurement (cm/mm) and the ability to interpret 2D/3D technical drawings (top, front, side views). Elementary 3
Tool Proficiency, Microcontrollers, and Fabrication
Students achieve proficiency with hand tools (calipers, multimeters, hot glue gun) and understand AC/DC current. They design automata and mechanical toys using mechanical principles.
Elementary 4
Theoretical Electronics and Text-Block Coding
Students master precise tool application and begin to understand Ohm's Law, conductors, and resistor codes (electronic theory). They start using a text-block language (Python), design and implement digital circuits with logic gates, and practice woodworking techniques (cutting, finishing, turning).
Elementary 5
Industrial Practices and Interactive Systems
Students apply safe workshop practices with hand and power tools (drills, soldering iron). They design complex 3D mechanisms (gears, transmissions) and are proficient in 3D printing workflow (mesh repair, optimization). Programming focuses on creating interactive systems using Arduino with digital/analog inputs/outputs, IF statements, and loops.

Key Focus Areas
Learning outcome
Elementary 1
Foundational Life & Physical Science
Students identify external body parts and the needs of living things (plants/animals), observing their interactions with nature. They explore basic matter properties (texture, weight), motion (pushes/pulls), and the cycles of day/night and weather, proposing simple environmental care actions.

Ecosystems, Earth Motion & Energy

Body Systems, Food Webs & Measurable Matter
Students understand the collaboration between the skeletal and muscular systems. They analyze food webs (producers, consumers) and Moon phases (rotation/translation). They learn to measure mass and length (using balances/rulers) and design simple machines using levers or pulleys.
Elementary 4
Geological Forces, Energy & Matter Change
Students explain how natural forces change Earth’s surface and design instruments to measure weather/climate. They differentiate between physical properties and chemical changes of matter and construct basic electric circuits.
Elementary 5
Cells, Density & Earth Formation
Students transition to microscopic study by distinguishing eukaryotic and prokaryotic cells. They investigate matter properties by calculating mass, volume, and density. They analyze Earth materials (rock formation, fossils) and model geological processes (weathering, erosion) and the water cycle.

Learning outcome

Newton's Laws, Gravity & Human Organization
Students apply Newton’s Laws of Motion (force, mass, acceleration) and explain how gravity affects objects on Earth and in space. Cellular Biology & Biomolecules
Students identify the four major biomolecules and their role in cellular metabolism. They identify main cell parts and their function, describe cellular respiration/photosynthesis, and explain how major human body systems maintain homeostasis (circulatory system pathway).

Atomic Structure & Chemical Reactions
Students define elements, compounds, and mixtures, and explain atomic structure (protons, neutrons, electrons). Middle school 9
Classical Physics: Motion, Energy & Electromagnetism
Students apply Newton’s three laws of motion to solve kinematic problems in 1D and 2D. They use conservation laws to analyze collisions and energy transformations, and they build and analyze electric circuits (current, voltage, resistance). They explain the principles of magnetism and electromagnetism and demonstrate their effects.

En el laboratorio de música y artes escénicas Docet, los estudiantes utilizan la música, danza y teatro para descubrir 
que cada sonido y movimiento pueden contar una historia. Empiezan jugando con el ritmo, moviéndose, explorando instrumentos y dejando que su imaginación marque el compás. 
Más adelante aprenden a tocar, crear y grabar sus propias canciones y coreografías, en un laboratorio lleno de guitarras, teclados, percusiones y otros materiales para explorar su creatividad.
A medida que avanzan, utilizan 
la inteligencia artificial 
para potenciar sus ideas y proyectos: grabando podcasts, produciendo música, creando representaciones artísticas y colaborando con otros laboratorios para crear experiencias únicas y 
divertidas.
En Docet, la música es el lenguaje que transmite una forma de pensar, de sentir y de transformar lo que los rodea.
