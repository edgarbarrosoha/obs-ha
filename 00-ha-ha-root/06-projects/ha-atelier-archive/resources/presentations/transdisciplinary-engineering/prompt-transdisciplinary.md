{What is TE?

TRANSDISCIPLINARY ENGINEERING 

Today’s complex problems, especially those that have a large impact on the environment and society, require a holistic approach to solving them by considering knowledge from a diverse range of disciplines, technical as well as social, and from user and practitioner communities. Interchange of such cross-disciplinary knowledge is needed to determine the overall environmental and societal impact. Many examples exist of engineering innovations that have a large adverse effect on the environment by not considering measures and regulations for mitigating these effects, for example, non-biodegradable plastics, certain pesticides and fertilizing products, use of greenhouse gasses, the long-term effect of medication and medical procedures, etc.

To create engineering solutions that minimize environmental and societal damage, engineering design and practices must involve people with the necessary knowledge and methods to determine the impact of new products and services, not only on client and user satisfaction but also on long-term sustainability. Designers and engineers need to come from a wide range of disciplines to achieve such sustainable solutions. The support of advanced ICT technology is deemed indispensable.

The International Society of Transdisciplinary Engineering (ISTE) aims to create awareness that engineering practices need to evolve into transdisciplinary engineering practices in which the exchange of different types of knowledge and people from a diverse range of disciplines is fundamental.

Transdisciplinary Engineering (TE) is the exchange of knowledge in the context of innovation, in product, process, organization, or social environment. Through ISTE, the exchange of knowledge is possible in its annual conferences, the TE Conferences, and through the publication of special issues in scientific journals.

{Transdisciplinary engineering is characterized by:

Integration beyond disciplinary boundaries - Unlike multi- or interdisciplinary approaches, transdisciplinary engineering transcends traditional disciplines to create new conceptual frameworks and methodologies.
Problem-centered approach - It focuses on complex real-world problems rather than disciplinary concerns, addressing challenges that cannot be solved within a single field.
Stakeholder involvement - It actively engages diverse stakeholders including users, experts from various domains, and affected communities in the problem-solving process.
Systems thinking - It employs holistic perspectives to understand interconnections, emergent properties, and feedback loops within complex systems.
Knowledge co-creation - It generates new knowledge through collaborative processes that combine academic expertise with practical and experiential knowledge.
Methodological pluralism - It draws on diverse research methods and epistemological approaches to address complex problems from multiple angles.
Reflexivity - It incorporates critical reflection on assumptions, values, and potential implications of engineering solutions.}
