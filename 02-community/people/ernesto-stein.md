# Ernesto Stein

• Professor of Public Policy at the School of Government and Public Transformation, Tecnológico de Monterrey (since Feb 2024).
• Former Representative of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group in Mexico (2021–2024), leading strategic initiatives to strengthen public policy and economic recovery in the country.
• Over 29 years of experience at the Inter-American Development Bank, serving as Principal Economist in the Research Department and Regional Economic Advisor for Central America, Mexico, Panama, and the Dominican Republic.
• Growth Fellow at Harvard’s Center for International Development (2006–2007).
• Holds a Ph.D. in Economics from the University of California, Berkeley and a Bachelor’s Degree in Economics from the University of Buenos Aires.
• Recognized for his contributions to the study of economic growth, macroeconomic stability, and public policy design in Latin America.
