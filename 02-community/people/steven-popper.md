# Steven Popper

• Distinguished University Professor of Decision Sciences at Tecnológico de Monterrey, School of Government and Public Transformation.
• Senior Economist at RAND Corporation with 37+ years of experience, and faculty member at Pardee RAND Graduate School since 1996.
• Served as Associate Director of the Science and Technology Policy Institute (1996–2002), supporting NSF and the Executive Office of the President.
• Former Country Account Officer at Bank of America, managing Eastern European operations.
• Ph.D. in Economics from University of California, Berkeley; B.Sc. in Biochemistry, Biophysics and Molecular Biology (Summa cum laude) from University of Minnesota.
• Extensive experience in policy analysis, science and technology policy, uncertainty analysis, and public sector decision-making.
