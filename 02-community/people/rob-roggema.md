# Rob Roggema

• Distinguished Professor of Regenerative Culture at Tecnológico de Monterrey, Mexico, and Founder of Cittaideale, a research office for adaptive planning and design.
• Visiting Professor at Queen’s University Belfast, focusing on climate-adaptive, nature-based, and food-resilient urban and rural landscapes.
• Renowned Landscape Architect with extensive experience in sustainable urbanism, climate adaptation, energy landscapes, and urban agriculture.
• Developer of Swarm Planning, a dynamic urban planning approach for climate change adaptation.
• Designed landmark projects including Edible Park (Ede), Moeder Zernike (Groningen), FoodRoofRio, and climate-resilient coastal interventions like Double Defence.
• Extensive international experience with design charrettes, community engagement, and advisory roles in Europe, Australia, Asia, and Latin America.
• Author/editor of multiple books on urban agriculture, climate adaptation, and regenerative urban design.
